Here’s an expanded list across multiple disciplines, covering fundamental principles, theories, and concepts that a well-rounded person should be familiar with:

---

### **Economics & Decision Science**

- **Supply and Demand** – Basic economic forces determining price and quantity.
- **Opportunity Cost** – The cost of the next best alternative when making a decision.
- **Comparative Advantage** – Basis of trade; countries benefit by specializing in what they produce most efficiently.
- **Tragedy of the Commons** – Overuse of shared resources due to individual incentives.
- **Prisoner's Dilemma** – A classic game theory problem about cooperation vs. self-interest.
- **Gresham’s Law** – "Bad money drives out good money."
- **Coase Theorem** – Property rights can solve externalities when transaction costs are low.
- **Nash Equilibrium** – A stable state in game theory where no player has an incentive to deviate.
- **Behavioral Economics** – How psychology affects economic decision-making.
- **Prospect Theory** – People evaluate potential losses and gains differently.

---

### **Philosophy & Ethics**

- **Occam’s Razor** – The simplest explanation is usually the best.
- **Hume’s Guillotine** – The distinction between "is" and "ought" in ethics.
- **Trolley Problem** – A moral dilemma about sacrificing one life to save many.
- **Categorical Imperative (Kant)** – Act according to maxims that could become universal laws.
- **Utilitarianism (Bentham, Mill)** – The greatest good for the greatest number.
- **Rawls' Veil of Ignorance** – Designing society without knowing one's own status.
- **Plato’s Cave** – A metaphor for perception and reality.
- **Nietzsche’s Will to Power** – The driving force behind human ambition.

---

### **Psychology & Cognitive Science**

- **Confirmation Bias** – The tendency to seek information that supports one's beliefs.
- **Dunning-Kruger Effect** – Incompetent people overestimate their ability.
- **Fundamental Attribution Error** – Overestimating personality traits and underestimating situational influences.
- **Stanford Prison Experiment** – How situational roles influence behavior.
- **Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs** – A framework for human motivation.
- **Freudian Defense Mechanisms** – Psychological strategies to protect the ego.
- **Skinner’s Operant Conditioning** – How behavior is shaped by reinforcement and punishment.

---

### **Mathematics & Statistics**

- **Law of Large Numbers** – Averages of large samples are more reliable.
- **Central Limit Theorem** – Many independent variables form a normal distribution.
- **Regression to the Mean** – Extreme results tend to revert to average over time.
- **Bayes’ Theorem** – Updating probabilities based on new evidence.
- **Chaos Theory** – Small changes in initial conditions can lead to huge consequences.
- **Gödel’s Incompleteness Theorems** – Some truths in mathematics cannot be proven.

---

### **Physics & Natural Sciences**

- **Newton’s Laws of Motion** – Foundation of classical mechanics.
- **Second Law of Thermodynamics** – Entropy (disorder) always increases.
- **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle** – One cannot precisely measure both position and momentum.
- **Relativity (Einstein)** – Space and time are relative to observers.
- **Schrödinger’s Cat** – Quantum superposition and measurement paradox.
- **Evolution by Natural Selection (Darwin)** – Survival of the fittest in biological adaptation.
- **Gaia Hypothesis** – Earth as a self-regulating system.

---

### **Technology & Computer Science**

- **Moore’s Law** – Computing power doubles approximately every two years.
- **Metcalfe’s Law** – The value of a network grows exponentially with its size.
- **Turing Completeness** – The ability of a system to simulate any computation.
- **Big-O Notation** – Describes algorithm efficiency.
- **CAP Theorem** – Distributed systems cannot simultaneously have consistency, availability, and partition tolerance.
- **Blockchain & Decentralization** – Distributed ledgers and trustless transactions.

---

### **History & Political Science**

- **Hegelian Dialectic** – Thesis, antithesis, and synthesis drive historical progress.
- **Montesquieu’s Separation of Powers** – Basis of modern democracy.
- **Realpolitik (Bismarck)** – Practical politics driven by power, not ideology.
- **The Peter Principle** – People are promoted until they reach their level of incompetence.
- **The Iron Law of Oligarchy** – All organizations, no matter how democratic, tend toward oligarchy.
- **The Cold War Balance of Power** – The geopolitical theory of deterrence.

---

### **Business & Management**

- **Porter’s Five Forces** – Framework for analyzing competitive advantage.
- **The Innovator’s Dilemma (Clayton Christensen)** – How disruptive innovation displaces incumbents.
- **The Peter Principle** – People rise to their level of incompetence in hierarchies.
- **The Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)** – 80% of results come from 20% of causes.
- **The Hawthorne Effect** – People alter behavior when observed.
