The Latticework of Ideas

### 1. **Psychology**

- **Biases and Heuristics**: Understanding cognitive biases like confirmation bias, anchoring, and availability heuristic.
- **Incentives**: Recognizing how incentives influence behavior.
- **Social Proof**: The impact of group behavior on individual decision-making.
- **Reciprocity**: The tendency to return favors.
- **Commitment and Consistency**: The drive to be consistent with one's commitments.

### 2. **Mathematics**

- **Probability**: Basic principles of probability and their application in risk assessment.
- **Statistics**: Understanding distributions, mean, variance, regression, etc.
- **Compound Interest**: The power of compounding over time.
- **Bayesian Thinking**: Updating probabilities as new information becomes available.

### 3. **Economics**

- **Supply and Demand**: The basics of how markets work.
- **Opportunity Cost**: The cost of forgoing the next best alternative.
- **Comparative Advantage**: The benefits of trade and specialization.
- **Incentives**: How incentives drive economic behavior.

### 4. **Biology**

- **Natural Selection**: The principles of evolution and adaptation.
- **Ecosystems**: Understanding complex interdependencies.
- **Biological Competition**: How competition shapes behavior and outcomes.

### 5. **Physics**

- **Critical Mass**: The point at which enough momentum builds to cause a self-sustaining process.
- **Thermodynamics**: Concepts like entropy and the conservation of energy.
- **Inertia**: The tendency of objects to resist changes in motion.

### 6. **History**

- **Cycles and Patterns**: Recognizing repeating patterns and cycles in history.
- **Causes and Consequences**: Understanding the causes and effects of historical events.

### 7. **Engineering**

- **Feedback Loops**: Positive and negative feedback mechanisms.
- **Redundancy and Failure Modes**: Designing systems to handle failures.
- **Complex adaptive systems**
- **Emergent behavior**
- **Chaotic systems**

### 8. **Sociology**

- **Networks**: The impact of social networks on behavior and outcomes.
- **Culture**: The role of culture in shaping behavior.

### 9. **Literature and Philosophy**

- **Ethical Frameworks**: Different approaches to ethics and morality.
- **Human Behavior**: Insights into human nature from great works of literature.

### 10. **Decision-Making**

- **Margin of Safety**: Always having a buffer in case things go wrong.
- **Second-Order Thinking**: Considering the longer-term and secondary consequences of actions.
- **Inversion**: Solving problems by thinking backwards.
